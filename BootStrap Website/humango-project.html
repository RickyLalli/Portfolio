Humango

Humango is a social dining app where you can plan
meetups to join other foodies at trying out new places together.

Introduction

Humango aims to connect like-minded individuals who are interested in experiencing food with others.

There is a stigma in Western culture that dictates that it's a negative to go out to eat alone. As a result we have created an application to allow users to enjoy the food they love without feeling isolated.

After all, good company enhances the experience of eating food, so why not get started today and explore the amazing food your city has to offer!

Discover new food

The Discover feature introduces a new way of finding places to eat.

Often, you're are forced go through a tedious process of finding a restaurant before being able to see what dishes are being offered.

However, as people who also occasionally want to pick out a new place quickly, we think it is important to allow users to choose restaurants directly by the images they're interested in.

Our Discover feed lets you scroll through images of key dishes to find your favourite places to try next.

Join other foodies

Once you're satisfied with choosing the right restaurant, you can join meetups that have been listed beneath the restaurant that you are are currently viewing.

Before joining, you are able to learn about the maximum group size, time, and date of the event.

Afterwards, you're placed within the group and have the opportunity as well to discuss any concerns or plans in the chat room prior to attending the meetup.

Host a meetup

Alternatively, if you can't find an existing meetup at a preferred location, you are able to host your own.

Meetup creations are limited to within the next 7 days to help users stay connected, and to prevent plans from turning stale.

Review your experience

After the meetup, feel free to rate and review the restaurant to let other foodies know about your experiences and adventures!

HUMANGO

Humango Application

The goal of this project was to build out an app, focusing primarily on interaction design over development. We build an app, which was focused on bringing people together to go out and explore new cuisine. Therefore, the design was focused on visuals to help motivate and social interactions. The design process began with brainstorming ideas for building a food app. After coming up with a general list of ideas, we began looking at current apps in the market which involved food and looking at the target audience. After researching a variety of applications, we decided there was a market for users wanting to visit restaurants, particularly in Vancouver, but going alone is not enjoyable and planning with friends limits people for this exploration.

 

Therefore, we set out to create an application where strangers could meet and try new foods, which would help stimulate conversations and appeal to those who are considered “foodies”.
